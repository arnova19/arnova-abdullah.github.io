I started on the other side of the classroom — teaching digital signal processing and network security to undergrads in Bangladesh, back when I'd read far more about spectrum analyzers than I'd actually touched one. That gap is what pulled me into lab and fieldwork: measuring 5G across a steep vineyard in Koblenz, syncing GPS to basebands at a mining site in Aachen, load-testing base stations in Dortmund.

Somewhere in this, the questions started outlasting the projects — a KPI that didn't behave the way the standard said it should, a sync error that only showed up underground. That's the itch that keeps pulling me toward the research side of every project: 5G/6G performance, industrial IoT, and network architecture, chased all the way down instead of stopping at "it works."

Publications

IEEE IWCMC 2026 · Shanghai, China
Risk-Aware and Stable Edge Server Selection Under Network Latency SLOs
Mohan Liyanage, Arnova Abdullah, Eldiyar Zhantileuov, Rolf Schuster.
Shows that picking edge servers by lowest average latency alone breaks down in shared, dynamic edge environments — servers get swapped back and forth as latency spikes and connections reset. Proposes a risk-aware selection method that accounts for latency variance and tail risk, keeping the selection stable while still meeting network latency SLOs.
2026 International Wireless Communications and Mobile Computing (IWCMC) DOI: 10.1109/IWCMC69287.2026.11580070 ↗
IEEE ICAC 2025 . Srilanka
Dynamic Edge Server Selection in Time-Varying Environments: A Reliability-Aware Predictive Approach
Jaime Sebastian Burbano, Arnova Abdullah, Eldiyar Zhantileuov, Mohan Liyanage, Rolf Schuster.
Proposes a predictive approach to selecting edge servers in environments where network conditions keep changing, factoring in server reliability over time rather than just current latency, to keep server selection stable as conditions shift.
2025 7th International Conference on Advancements in Computing (ICAC) DOI: 10.1109/ICAC69156.2025.11361485 ↗
